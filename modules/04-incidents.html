Module 04

Incident Response

Respond to outages with clear roles, fast communication, and structured learning afterward.

Incident Severity Levels

  • SEV1 — Complete outage or data loss affecting all users. All-hands response.
  • SEV2 — Major degradation affecting a significant user segment.
  • SEV3 — Minor impact, workaround available. Business-hours response.
  • SEV4 — Low impact, tracked but not urgent.

Incident Roles

  • Incident Commander (IC) — Coordinates response, makes decisions, shields responders from distractions.
  • Operations Lead — Drives technical investigation and remediation.
  • Communications Lead — Updates stakeholders, status page, and support teams.
  • Scribe — Documents timeline, actions, and decisions in real time.
One person, one role Don't let the IC also debug the system. Separation keeps coordination clear under pressure.

Response Workflow

  1. Detect — Alert fires or user report arrives.
  2. Triage — Assess severity, assign IC, open incident channel.
  3. Mitigate — Restore service first (rollback, failover, scale up). Root cause can wait.
  4. Resolve — Confirm recovery, monitor for recurrence.
  5. Postmortem — Blameless review within 48 hours.

Communication Template

INCIDENT UPDATE — SEV2 — [Service Name]
Status:   Investigating | Mitigating | Resolved
Impact:   [Who is affected and how]
Started:  [Time UTC]
Actions:  [What we're doing now]
Next update: [Time]

Blameless Postmortems

The goal is to learn, not to punish. A good postmortem includes:

  • Timeline — What happened, minute by minute
  • Root cause — The systemic reason, not "human error"
  • Impact — Users affected, duration, SLO budget consumed
  • What went well — Celebrate effective responses
  • Action items — Specific, assigned, with due dates
Remember Humans make mistakes because systems allowed them to. Fix the system, not the person.
